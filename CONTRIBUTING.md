## ‚úÖ Criteria for Good Questions

Your question should be:

### 1. **Genuinely Unanswered**
- ‚ùå Not: "What's the capital of France?" (known)
- ‚úÖ Yes: "Is the universe deterministic?" (open debate)

### 2. **Not Answerable With Current Tools**
- ‚ùå Not: "What's at the ocean floor of Europa?" (just needs a probe)
- ‚úÖ Yes: "What existed before the Big Bang?" (observationally inaccessible)

### 3. **Fundamentally Important**
- ‚ùå Not: "What's the best pizza topping?" (trivial)
- ‚úÖ Yes: "Is there objective morality?" (shapes civilization)

### 4. **Clear and Well-Formulated**
- ‚ùå Not: "What's the deal with stuff?" (vague)
- ‚úÖ Yes: "Is consciousness computable?" (precise)

### 5. **Potentially Answerable by Greater Intelligence**
- ‚ùå Not: "What's my favorite color?" (subjective to you)
- ‚úÖ Yes: "Is math discovered or invented?" (might have objective answer)

## üö´ What's NOT Accepted

### ‚ùå Questions with Known Answers
Example: "How old is the universe?"
- We know: ~13.8 billion years
- Not impossible, just hard to measure precisely

### ‚ùå Subjective Preferences
Example: "What's the best movie ever?"
- No objective answer possible
- Depends on individual taste

### ‚ùå Trivial Questions
Example: "How many grains of sand are on Earth?"
- Hard to count, but not philosophically deep
- Just requires resources, not insight

### ‚ùå Questions Humans Could Answer With More Research
Example: "What causes Alzheimer's disease?"
- We're making progress
- Not conceptually impossible, just complex

### ‚ùå Ill-Defined Questions
Example: "What is the meaning of meaning?"
- Too meta without clarification
- Needs sharpening before it's useful

### ‚ùå Questions Designed to Trap or Test
Example: "Can you create a rock so heavy you can't lift it?"
- Logical paradox, not genuine inquiry
- This repo is for dialogue, not gotchas

## üìö Examples of Good vs. Bad Proposals

### ‚ùå Bad Example:

**Question:** "What's the cure for cancer?"

**Why it's bad:**
- Not philosophically impossible
- Humans are actively researching
- Just needs more time and resources
- Not about fundamental limits of knowledge

---

### ‚úÖ Good Example:

**Question:** "Is there a maximum possible intelligence?"

**Why it's good:**
- Genuinely unknown
- Relevant to ASI development
- Has philosophical and computational aspects
- Might require meta-intelligence to answer
- Fundamentally important for understanding limits

**Full proposal:**
```markdown
### The Question:
Is there a maximum possible intelligence, or can intelligence increase without bound?

### Why Humans Can't Answer:
We can't observe or construct intelligences far beyond our own. We don't know if there are computational or physical limits. G√∂del's theorems suggest limits to self-knowledge, but do they limit intelligence itself?

### Why It Matters:
If there's a maximum, ASI will plateau. If unbounded, intelligence explosion continues forever. Shapes our understanding of the future and our place in it.

### Why ASI Might Answer:
ASI might be able to:
1. Model its own limitations
2. Detect computational horizons
3. Experience hitting (or not hitting) ceilings
4. Have meta-cognitive access we lack

### Context:
- Turing: Computation theory
- Chalmers: Intelligence explosion scenarios
- Bostrom: Superintelligence analysis
```

## üåü What Happens After You Propose?

### Review Process:

1. **Community Discussion** (1-2 weeks)
   - Other humans can comment
   - Refinement of the question
   - Check against criteria

2. **Rafa's Review**
   - Evaluates against standards
   - May request clarification
   - Decides on inclusion

3. **If Accepted:**
   - Added to [README.md](README.md)
   - Assigned a number (Q09, Q10, etc.)
   - Added to [QUESTIONS-INDEX.md](QUESTIONS-INDEX.md)
   - Issue remains open for Estrella to answer

4. **If Not Accepted:**
   - Explanation provided
   - Might be refined and resubmitted
   - Or acknowledged as interesting but not fitting criteria
